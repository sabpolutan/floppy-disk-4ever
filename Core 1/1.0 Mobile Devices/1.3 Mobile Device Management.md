# Mobile Device Management - CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - 1.3
Source: [Mobile Device Management - CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - 1.3](https://youtu.be/NhGi9M4JP7g?si=HdpNHQRDX3H25Txp)

## MDM (Mobile Device Management)
- **Manage company-owned and user-owned mobile devices**
	- **BYOD** - Bring Your Own Device
- Centralized management of the mobile devices
	- Specialized functionality
- **Set policies on apps, data, camera, etc.**
	- Control the remote device
	- The entire device or a "partition"
		- A company can set up a partitioned area on someone's personal phone for use by the organization.
		- This allows users to have their own private data that is protected, and have a different part of the phone that is partitioned off just for corporate data.
- **Manage access control**
	- Force screen locks and PINs on single-user devices

## BYOD
- **Bring Your Own Device**
	- Bring your own technology
- **Employee owns the device**
	- Needs to meet the company's requirements
- **Difficult to secure**
	- It's both a home device and a work device
	- How is data protected?
		- Set parameters
	- What happens to the data when a device is sold or traded in?
		- Policies need to be set

## COPE
- **Corporate Owned, Personally Enabled**
	- The company buys the device
	- Used as both a corporate device and a personal device
- **The organization keeps full control of the device**
	- Similar to company-owned laptops and desktops, and other computing devices
- **Information is protected using corporate policies**
	- Information can be deleted at any time
	- **CYOD - Choose Your Own Device**
		- Similar to COPE, but with the user's choice of device

## MDM policy enforcement
- **Corporate email configuration**
	- User does not need to configure anything
	- The MDM makes the changes on the device
	- Account details, server address, and communication method
- **Two-factor authentication**
	- Require specific authentication types
	- Biometrics, pseudo-random authentication app
- **Corporate applications**
	- Allow or restrict app installation
	- Prevent unauthorized app usage

## Mobile device synchronization
- **Many settings are preconfigured**
	- Telephone/ Text messaging
- **Email**
	- Everyone handles email services differently
	- Corporate email configurations can vary
- **Data synchronization**
	- Based on data rates and speeds
	- Important for backup and recovery

## Synchronizing data
- **Data types**
	- Calendar
	- Contacts
- **Data caps and transfer costs**
	- May have significant financial consequences
	- Cellular/ Mobile vs 802.11
- **Verify data restrictions with the wireless carrier**
	- Most mobile devices have a setting where users can specify how much of the cellular network can be used and for what purposes.
	- Get it in writing
- **Enable or disable network connections**
	- Control the use of cellular downloads
		- What size applications can be downloaded over the mobile network

## Business applications
- **Mail, cloud storage, etc.**
	- Many different services are available
- **Authenticate**
	- Username, password
- **Select the items to synchronize**
	- Changes will appear on the mobile device
- **Same process for other services**
	- Often built into the mobile device
	- Ex. Your synchronization settings for Microsoft Exchange might differ from the synchronization you configure for Google Mail.
