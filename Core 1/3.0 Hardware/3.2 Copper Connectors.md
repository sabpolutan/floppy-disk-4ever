## RJ11 connector
![515](<./assets/3.2 Copper Connectors/file-20260307163304232.png>)[^1]
- Relatively common
- **6 position, 2 conductors (6P2C)**
	- Some cables will wire additional conductors
	- Sometimes there are 4 wires or 4 conductors, making it an RJ14 connector.
- **Telephone or DSL connection**
	- Analog telephone
- In a 6P2C setup, the two wires used are typically the *Center Tips*. If you're looking at old-school wiring colors, that would be the *Red and Green* wires.

## RJ45 connector
![](<./assets/3.2 Copper Connectors/file-20260307165932729.png>)[^2]
- **Registered Jack type 45**
- **8 position, 8 conductors (8P8C)**
	- Modular connector
	- Commonly used for Ethernet connection
	- Can also be used for serial connections

![](<./assets/3.2 Copper Connectors/file-20260307170309022.png>)[^3]
- **RJ11 and RJ45 slightly differ in size**
	- RJ11 is smaller.
	- This can make it very easy to make a mistake if you're reaching behind a device to plug things in.
	- You could accidentally plug in an RJ11 connector to an RJ45 connection.


### RJ "Family" Comparison

|**Connector**|**Common Use**|**Typical Configuration**|
|---|---|---|
|**RJ11**|Standard Telephone|**6P2C**|
|**RJ14**|Dual-line Telephone|**6P4C**|
|**RJ45**|Ethernet Networking|**8P8C**|

## F-connector
![](<./assets/3.2 Copper Connectors/file-20260307172720216.png>)[^4]
- **Coax cable**
	- Threaded connector
- **Cable television**
	- Cable modem
	- DOCSIS (Data Over Cable Service Interface Specification)


## Punchdown block
- **Wire-to-wire patch panel**
	- No intermediate interface required
- **Wires are "punched" into the block**
	- Connecting block is on top
	- Relatively inexpensive approach to connecting wires
- **Additional wires punched into connecting block**
	- Patch the top to the bottom

> A **Punchdown Block** is a type of terminal block used to connect network or phone wires, where individual wires are "punched down" into slots to establish a secure connection. 
> - [Star Telecom](https://www.startelecom.ca/glossary/punchdown-block/)

### USB-C
![](<./assets/3.2 Copper Connectors/file-20260307180519165.png>)[^8]
- **24-pin double-sided USB connector**
	- Used for both hosts and devices
	- Encapsulated all of the different connector types into one single type of USB connector
- **Used for USB, Thunderbolt**
	- Interface is the same, signal can vary


## Molex connector
![422](<./assets/3.2 Copper Connectors/file-20260307181108085.png>)[^9]
- **4-pin peripheral power connector**
	- Created by Molex Connector Company
	- AMP Mate-n-Lok
	- Provides +12 V and +5 V
- **Power for many devices in the computer case**
	- Storage devices
	- Fans
	- Power supply
	- Other peripherals

## Lightning
![431](<./assets/3.2 Copper Connectors/file-20260307181631806.png>)[^10]
- **Apple propriety**
	- 8-pin digital signals
	- iPhone, iPad, iPod devices
- **Some advantages over Micro-USB**
	- Higher power output for phones and tablets
	- Can be inserted either way

## DB-9
- **See also [3.2 Peripheral Cables](<./3.2 Peripheral Cables.md>)**
- **D-subminiature or D-sub**
	- The letter refers to the connector size
- **Commonly used for RS-232**
	- Recommended Standard 232
	- An industry standard since 1969
- **Serial communications standard**
	- Built for modem communication
	- Used for modems, printers, mice, and networking
- **Now used as a configuration port**
	- Management or Console port
