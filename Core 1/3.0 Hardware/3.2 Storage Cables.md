## SATA (Serial AT Attachment)
- **SATA Revision 1.0**
	- SATA 1.5 Gbit/s, 1 meter
- **SATA Revision 2.0**
	- SATA 3.0 Gbit/s, 1 meter
- **SATA Revision 3.0**
	- SATA 6.0 Gbit/s, 1 meter
- **SATA Revision 3.2**
	- SATA 16 Gbit/s, 1 meter
- **eSATA (external SATA)**
	- Matches the SATA version
	- 2 meters

![](<./assets/3.2 Storage Cables/file-20260306213738626.png>)[^1]
- **SATA structure**
	- The standard supports two physical connections
	- The *15-pin* connector is used for power
	- A *7-pin* connector is used for the data

![](<./assets/3.2 Storage Cables/file-20260306214502017.png>)[^2]

![577](<./assets/3.2 Storage Cables/file-20260306215001733.png>)[^3]
- **One power cable and a data cable per device**
	- One-to-one relationship between the connector on the motherboard and the connector on the storage drive.
	- Does not support any type of daisy chaining or multiple drives on a single cable
		- This makes it easy to understand
	- If there are about 8 SATA interfaces on the motherboard,
		- It can support 8 total drives

## eSATA cable
![](<./assets/3.2 Storage Cables/file-20260306215740047.png>)[^4]
- **External device connections**
	- Uses the SATA standard
	- Different types of connectors
- The signal sent across the wire is effectively the same between the internal and external drive
	- However, the connector itself is slightly different between the two.
![612](<./assets/3.2 Storage Cables/file-20260306220016720.png>)[^5]
- **The SATA is always on the inside of the computer**
	- Has a distinctive L-shape 
![](<./assets/3.2 Storage Cables/file-20260306220430862.png>)[^6]
- **The eSATA is always outside of the computer**
	- The "e" stands for "external"
	- You'll need an exclusive cable for the eSATA drive
		- It will obviously not work with a SATA standard cable

## Key Differences: SATA vs. eSATA

| **Feature**         | **SATA (Internal)**                            | **eSATA (External)**                                  |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| **Location**        | Inside the computer case.                      | Outside the computer case.                            |
| **Connector Shape** | "L" shaped (keyed) to prevent wrong insertion. | Straight, rectangular shape with no "L" hook.         |
| **Durability**      | Delicate; designed for few "plug-ins."         | Rugged; designed for thousands of plug/unplug cycles. |
| **Cable Length**    | Limited to **1 meter**.                        | Extended up to **2 meters**.                          |
| **Shielding**       | Unshielded (assumes the PC case blocks RF).    | Heavily shielded to prevent **RF interference**.      |
