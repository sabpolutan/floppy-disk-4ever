## LCD
- **Liquid crystal display**
	- Light shines through liquid crystals
- **Advantages**
	- Lightweight
	- Relatively low power
	- Relatively inexpensive
- **Disadvantages**
	- Black levels are a challenge
	- Requires a separate backlight
		- Fluorescent, **LED (Light Emitting Diodes)**, etc.
		- Lights are difficult to replace

## LCD technologies
- **TN (Twisted Nematic) LCD**
	- The original LCD technology
	- Fast response times (gaming)
	- Poor viewing angles - color shifts
- **IPS (In-Plane Switching) LCD**
	- Excellent color representation
	- Can be more expensive to produce than TN
- **VS (Vertical Alignment) LCD**
	- A good compromise between TN and IPS
	- Good color representation
	- Often slower response times than TN

## OLED
- **Organic Light Emitting Diode**
	- An organic compound emits light when receiving an electric current
- **No backlight**
	- The organic compound provides the light
- **Thinner and lighter**
	- Flexible and mobile - no glass needed
- **Tablets, phones, smart watches**
	- Very accurate color representation
	- Tends to be a bit higher cost than LCD

## Mini LED
- **Same backlight technology as a conventional LED-backlit LCD**
	- But much smaller Light Emitting Diodes (LEDs)
- **Each LED can be enabled or disabled**
	- And color and intensity can be different
- **Much better control over the dark screen areas**
	- Deeper blacks, better color representation

## Touchscreen
- **Merge laptop and tablet input**
	- Digitizer responds to touch
	- No keyboard required
	- But often still available
- **Many options for input**
	- Use the best one for the job
- **Use a pen-like device as input**
	- Stylus input
	- Useful for graphical input
- **Used commonly on laptops/tablets**
	- Or hybrid devices

## Backlight and inverter
- **LCD displays need a backlight**
	- Fluorescent lamp or LED lights
- **Some laptops have inverters**
	- Turn *DC (Direct current)* into *AC (Alternating current)*
	- Power for LED requires DC, which is used to power the laptop
	- Older backlight style of LCD, like fluorescent lamps require AC
		- Laptops will need to invert the current to use fluorescent backlights
- **Verify backlight**
	- Look closely
	- Use a flashlight
		- Shine it directly at the screen so that the light will reflect from the back of the display itself.
- **May need to replace the LCD inverter or display**
	- Choose carefully
		- Check the manufacturer documentation on what backlights your device use
	- If the display of text or graphics is faint on the screen, the problem could be due to the backlight.
	- If the issue involves the fluorescent backlight, you might need to replace the built-in inverter.
		- The inverters are usually found in the bezel under the display itself.
