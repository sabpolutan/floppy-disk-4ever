## Structured cabling standards
- **International ISO/IEC 11801 cabling standards**
	- Defines classes of networking standards
- **Telecommunications Industry Association (TIA)**
	- Standards, market analysis, trade shows, government affairs, etc.
	- ANSI/TIA-568: Commercial Building Telecommunications Cabling Standard
	- [Telecommunications Industry Association - Official Website](https://tiaonline.org/)
- **Commonly referenced for pin and pair assignments of eight-conductor 100-ohm balanced twisted pair cabling**
	- T568A and T568B

## T568A and T568B termination
![](<./assets/3.2 568A and 568B Colors/file-20260306150134227.png>)[^1]
- **Pin assignments from T56B standard**
	- Eight-conductor 100-ohm balanced twisted pair cabling
- **T568A and T568B are different pin assignments for 8P8C connectors**
	- The colors are just slightly different between the two
	- Both are used on the same RJ45 type connection
- **Many organizations traditionally use 568B**
	- Difficult to change mid-stream
	- Organizations are normally consistent with their wiring standards
- **You shouldn't terminate one side of the cable with T56A and the other with T56B**
	- This has never been the definition of a Gigabit Ethernet crossover cable

![](<./assets/3.2 568A and 568B Colors/file-20260306151036015.png>)[^2]
- Understanding color schemes is also important when you walk into any network where you may need to perform crimping or cable management.
- T568A pins 1 and 2 are white and green, green, and pins 3 and 6 are white and orange, orange.
- T568B pins 1 and 3 have white and orange, orange, and pins 3 and 6 are white and green, green.
- *The pins on 4 and 5, and 7 and 8 are the same between the two pins*.
![](<./assets/3.2 568A and 568B Colors/file-20260306153439977.png>)[^3]

#### Notes to help remember the T568A and T568B pins
- Every odd number is half white. 
	- A(pple) Green / B(old) Orange 
- Blues are in the middle
- Browns are always last
